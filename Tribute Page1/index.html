Sachin Tendulkar

24th April 1973

The God Of Cricket

Though everyone names Sachin Tendulkar as the “God of Cricket” many don’t know the real reason behind this. The legendary Indian cricketer took up cricket at the age of eleven. On 14 November 1987, the 14-year-old Tendulkar was selected to represent Bombay in the Ranji Trophy, India’s premier domestic first-class cricket tournament, for the 1987–88 season. In 1992, at the age of 19, Tendulkar became the first overseas-born player to represent Yorkshire, which prior to Tendulkar joining the team, never selected players even from other English countries. Making a century or a double century in ODI and Test may look easier to this generation of players. But during Sachin’s young cricketing carrier he played against many legendary bowlers in a tough turf condition. Eventually, the God of cricket Sachin Tendulkar also called as “Master Blaster”, and “Little Master”. Legend himself Brian Lara termed Sachin Tendulkar as “Bradman of modern era”.

Achievemants

  • Made his debut for the Indian cricket team in November 1989 at the age of 16.
  • Became the youngest player to score a century in international cricket at the age of 17 against England in 1990.
  • Scored the first-ever double century in One Day Internationals (ODIs) in 2010 against South Africa.
  • Holds the record for the highest run-scorer in both Test and ODI cricket.
  • Achieved the remarkable feat of scoring 100 international centuries (ODIs and Tests combined).
  • Played a crucial role in India's 2011 Cricket World Cup victory, contributing significantly with the bat.
  • Awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 2014.
  • Won the ICC Cricketer of the Year award multiple times, receiving the Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy.
  • Became the first player to reach 10,000 runs in One Day Internationals.
